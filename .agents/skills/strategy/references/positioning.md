# Positioning

Use when choosing a PMF wedge, target segment, product category, pricing shape, channel, or user-facing positioning.

## PMF Wedge

PMF is not "serve the whole future market." It is finding a sharp entry point with high early ROI.

A good wedge is:

- narrow enough to serve deeply
- painful or urgent enough that users tolerate rough edges
- enabled by a recent change in technology, cost, supply, demand, distribution, or behavior
- expandable into a larger market
- measurable through behavior, not praise

Bad signs:

- broad segment, weak pain
- users like the idea but do not change behavior
- team spends heavily before the wedge is proven
- team finds a wedge but does not increase intensity

## Innovation Adoption

Match product quality and messaging to adoption stage.

| Stage | Product implication |
| --- | --- |
| Innovators | Simple, rough, effective; prove the core breakthrough. |
| Early adopters | Improve usability, trust, and repeatability. |
| Early majority | Reduce risk, explain category, add reliability and proof. |
| Late majority | Optimize convenience, price, support, and compatibility. |

Early products should not try to satisfy late-majority expectations unless the team has enough resources.

## STP

Use STP to find the wedge:

- **Segment**: split by real differences in demand, urgency, willingness to pay, context, channel, or operating requirement.
- **Target**: choose the segment with the best early ROI, not necessarily the largest segment.
- **Positioning**: define what the product is in the user's mind and why this target should choose it now.

Good positioning connects:

- target user
- urgent need
- category or alternative
- differentiated promise
- reason to believe

Separate target user from positioning. The target is who; positioning is what this is and why it wins in their mind.

## 4P

Use 4P as a connected system:

1. Price
2. Product
3. Place
4. Promotion

Pricing shapes demand and therefore product. Do not design the product in isolation and price afterward.

Ask:

- What price point or cost level unlocks demand?
- What product can be delivered profitably and credibly at that price?
- What channel reaches the target segment efficiently?
- What promotion creates the right user understanding with the lowest cost?
